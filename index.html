Esmaeil Mousavi

Biography

Esmaeil Mousavi is an innovative researcher and educator specializing in artificial intelligence, machine learning, and autonomous systems. Currently pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science at Weber State University in Ogden, UT, he is deeply involved in advancing research in areas such as cybersecurity, computer vision, and data science. His work spans interdisciplinary projects—from autonomous vehicle technologies and swarm learning models to environmental data analysis—underscoring his commitment to both academic excellence and practical innovation.

Work Experience

Position Institution Duration Responsibilities
Assistant Researcher: ML in Acoustic Denial and Cybersecurity Weber State University Jan 2023 – Present
  • Conducted over 100 acoustic denial attack simulations on hard disk drives (HDDs), significantly reducing noise-related failures by implementing an ML-driven noise cancellation mechanism.
  • Secured a $5,000 research grant to present findings at the National Conference on Undergraduate Research in April 2025.
Graduate Assistant Teacher: CS 6300 – Route Planning and Navigation Weber State University March 2023 – Dec 2023
  • Co-developed and delivered a graduate-level course for seven students, awarded for interdisciplinary work between Computer Science and Automotive Technology departments.
  • Led hands-on sessions and projects on autonomous systems; programming TurtleBots, InDro bots, and F1-Tenth Racing cars.
Assistant Researcher: AI and Autonomous Vehicles Weber State University Oct 2022 – Present
  • Advanced self-driving technologies using Torch, OpenCV, 3D LiDARs, and SWARM learning models for decentralized and collaborative obstacle recognition and vehicle behavior projects.
  • Hosted tours and guest lectures for 25+ participants, increasing student interest in autonomous systems by 30%.
Assistant Researcher National Center for Water Quality Research Nov 2021 – Sept 2022
  • Applied ML to predict water quality trends in the Sandusky River 8 years ahead using a 10-year dataset providing actionable insights for environmental decision-making in collaboration with Broad Institute of MIT and Harvard.
Data Analyst National Center for Water Quality Research Dec 2021 – Sept 2022
  • Processed and visualized river sensor data, creating reports to support environmental water quality monitoring.
